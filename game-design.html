Game Design


This is my page for dumping any design ideas that are at least mostly coherent. I'll never claim that any of them are great — or even entirely original, for that matter — but the only way I know to practice is to pitch ideas into the void and explain my thought process behind them.

Scholar

This is a homebrew class I made for D&D 5e. I've both played it myself and had a few friends give it a try as well with generally positive results.
Highlighted portions contain design notes.

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d6 per scholar level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 6 + your Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per scholar level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: None
Weapons: Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows
Tools: None
Saving Throws: Intelligence, Wisdom
Skills: Choose two from Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, and Religion

Equipment

  • (a) a quarterstaff or (b) a dagger
  • (a) a component pouch or (b) an arcane focus
  • (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
  • A Primordial Quill

Spellcasting

Years of devoted study have enabled you to tap into magical energy and use it to cast spells. You use the wizard spell list and all spells you learn from this class count as wizard spells for you.

Your tendency to specialize heavily in specific areas of study manifests in you knowing fewer spells than most spellcasters of a comparable level.

Cantrips

At 1st level, you know two cantrips of your choice from the wizard spell list. You learn additional wizard cantrips of your choice at higher levels, as shown in the Cantrips Known column of the Scholar table.

Spell Slots

The Scholar table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your spells of 1st level and higher. To cast one of these wizard spells, you must expend a slot of the spell's level or higher. You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a long rest.

For example, if you know the 1st-level spell Burning Hands and have a 1st-level and a 2nd-level spell slot available, you can cast Burning Hands using either slot.

Spells Known of 1st Level and Higher

You prepare the list of wizard spells that are available for you to cast. To do so, choose a number of wizard spells from the wizard spell list equal to your Intelligence modifier + 1/3 your Scholar level (rounded down, minimum of one spell). The spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

When you complete a long rest, you may replace one of the wizard spells you know with another wizard spell of a level for which you have spell slots.

Spellcasting Ability

Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for your wizard spells, since the power of your magic relies on your ability to study and memorize the intricacies of magic. You use your Intelligence whenever a spell refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use your Intelligence modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a wizard spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier
Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

Spellcasting Focus

You can use an arcane focus or your Primordial Quill as a spellcasting focus for your wizard spells.

Ritual Casting

You can cast a wizard spell as a ritual if that spell has the ritual tag and you have the spell prepared.

Primordial Quill

You carry a Primordial Quill which you can use a spellcasting focus for your wizard spells. Through your studies, you have learned to write powerful words of primordial power that manipulate the world around you. You can learn two Primordial Words in total and additional ones at levels 6 and 13.

Primordial Words are split into two categories: Glyphs and Incantations.

You require a charge to write a Glyph or speak an Incantation. You have a number of charges equal to your 2 + your Proficiency Bonus. You regain all charges when finishing a long rest.

At 1st Level, you gain access to Glyphs. Glyphs require 1 minute to write due to their intricacy, during which time you must remain focused and use your Primordial Quill to draw the glyph from memory. You may choose from the following Glyphs:

  • Glyph of Piercing Vision: When drawn onto a surface, this glyph can affect a circular area up to 5ft in radius. All creatures on the side of the surface on which the glyph was drawn can see through to the other side, provided that the surface is no more than 5ft thick and has no anti-magic properties. The glyph remains active for up to 1 hour and can be dismissed by the caster at will.
  • Glyph of Warding Force: After being drawn onto a surface, this glyph can be activated by the caster as a Bonus Action or Reaction to create a wall of force up to 5ft in two directions and up to 15ft tall from the location the glyph was drawn on. The wall of force can only run along the surface, otherwise it will not appear. The wall persists for 1 minute. During that time, any creature which sends a projectile through the wall must make an Intelligence Saving Throw against your Spell Save DC to avoid having the projectile be destroyed on contact with the wall. In addition, any creature which attempts to pass through the wall must make a Constitution Saving Throw against Spell Save DC. If they fail the save, their speed becomes 0 until the start of their next turn, and are knocked prone.
  • Glyph of Anchoring: When a creature moves within 5ft of this glyph, the glyph activates, Paralyzing them for up to 1 minute. During that time, they can attempt a Wisdom Saving Throw against your Spell Save DC at the end of each of their turns, ending the effect on a success. If the target is immune to being Paralyzed or of a size greater than Large, they are instead Restrained for the same duration. You can specify any number of creatures to be immune to the glyph when you first draw it.
At 5th level, you gain access to Incantations. Incantations can be spoken at a moment's notice.
  • Word of Shielding:As a Reaction when a creature you can see within 60ft of you is hit by an attack or spell attack, you utter a word of power and rewrite the brief period of history leading to the hit, forcing the attacker to reroll their attack.
  • Word of Annuling:As a Bonus Action, select a creature you can see within 60ft and utter a word of power that compels a spell or magical effect affecting them to end immediately. If the spell is of Spell Slot Level 3 or higher, you must expend a Spell Slot of equal level to end the effect.
  • Word of Intangibility:As a Bonus Action, select any number of objects within a 5ft cube and turn them intangible. This incantation can only affect objects weighing less than 50 pounds. Affected objects remain floating at the location they were at when the incantation was used. Creatures and other objects move through them as though they were not there. If an object is held by a creature, that creature may make a Dexterity Saving Throw against your Spell Save DC to avoid this effect on an item of their choice. The objects remain intangible for 1 minute or until you willingly end the effect at any point on your turn.

Swiftwriting

Starting at 2nd level, your scholarly endeavors have trained you to write and read at an almost unnatural pace when needed. You are able to garner a summary of a text of almost any length near instantly. If you are able to spend at least 1 minute studying a text, image, speech, etc. then you can recall it with perfect accuracy for up to 1 month afterwards.

In addition, when finishing a short or long rest, you may choose one Glyph and prepare ahead of time to write it quickly when needed. When you write the prepared Glyph, you can choose to do so as a Bonus Action rather than the usual writing time. You can use this ability once and regain the ability to do so when you finish a short or long rest.

Field of Study

Starting at 3rd level, you select a field of study in which to focus your research. See the bottom of the document for a list of fields to choose from.

Sage's Memory

Starting at 10th level, your detailed study has granted you insight into countless fields. When you perform an Insight, History, or Arcana Check, you may choose to automatically succeed. You must choose to use this ability before you roll for the check. Once you use this ability, you cannot do so again until you finish a long rest.

Primeval Speech

Starting at 18th level, you may learn Power Word: Stun or Power Word: Kill and always have that spell prepared. You may change your choice when finishing a long rest.

In addition, you can communicate using Primeval Speech for up to 1 minute. During this time, the meaning behind your words is comprehensible to all creatures, even those normally incapable of understanding language. In addition, you gain advantage on Intimidation, Persuasion, and Deception checks during that time. Once you use this ability, you cannot do so again until you finish a short or long rest.

Revision

At 20th level, the nature of existence itself is laid bare to you. When you witness an event, you may, immediately after it occurs, bend the universe itself to your will and undo the event. An event could be a creature casting a spell, an ally slipping from a cliffside, or any other discrete occurrence that your DM permits. The extreme effort required to perform this act drains your strength. You suffer one level of Exhaustion immediately after use. Once you use this ability, you cannot do so again until you finish a long rest.

Untitled Support

This is a League of Legends character idea designed for the support role. Balance not included.

For some abilities, I provide alternate ideas that I've considered alongside the main ones. Design comments (on highlighted portions) help explain my reasoning.

Passive - Conduit

Draining Touch: When not on cooldown, your next basic attack gains extended range (roughly 450 units) and drains the target, slowing them briefly, granting you 1 Charge, and Marking them for a few seconds. If an ally damages a Marked enemy, the enemy takes an additional burst of damage.

Cooldown: Low-Moderate (Roughly 16-8s, scaling with level)

Charge: You can store up to 4 Charge at a time. Charge persists while out of combat. When you have 4 Charge stored, your next cast of Surge [or the alternate ability Reinforce] is empowered.

Supercharged: Some of your abilities grant you and your allies Supercharged. This effect grants the following bonuses:

  • Increased movement speed
  • Increased attack speed, allowing exceeding the attack speed cap
These bonuses last 3s. Landing a basic attack on an enemy champion resets the duration to a minimum of 2s.

When you are empowered with Supercharged, you are unable to gain Charge but gain the benefits of Draining Touch on every basic attack in addition to the buffs listed above.

Q - Siphon / Displace [Alternate Recast: Warp Strike]

Siphon: Throw out a skillshot which pierces minions but stops at the first enemy champion hit. On hit, you gain 1 Charge and the target takes damage and is tethered for 2s. During that time, you can access to Displace. If the tether persists until the end of the duration, you gain an additional 1 Charge.

Displace: Pull the tethered target a short distance towards your cursor, applying Draining Touch's Mark, and ending the tether immediately.

[Alternate] Warp Strike: An alternate ability to replace Displace. Blink behind the tethered champion, slowing them and applying Draining Touch's mark. Using this effect greatly increases the cooldown of this ability.

Cooldown: Low-Moderate (Roughly 14-8s)
Mana Cost: Low-Moderate

W - Surge [Alternate: Reinforce]

Surge: Consume all Charge and grant the target ally and yourself a shield. The strength of the shield is increased for each unit of Charge consumed. If 4 units of Charge are consumed, the target ally and yourself are Supercharged.

Cooldown: Low-Moderate (Roughly 12-8s)
Mana Cost: Moderate

[Alternate] Reinforce: Dash to the target ally champion and perform a circular sweep shortly afterwards which damages enemies and knocks them back a short distance. You can target yourself with this ability to perform the sweep at your current location. Casting Rush or United Charge during the dash or sweep cancels the sweep and immediately performs that ability instead.
If you have 4 Charge, all Charge is consumed to instead cast Empowered Reinforce. You also cast Empowered Reinforce if you are Supercharged at the time of casting, though doing so does not refresh the duration of Supercharge.
Empowered Reinforce: Dash to the target ally champion and create a powerful blast which damages and knocks away nearby enemies, stunning them for a short time. You and the target ally then become Supercharged. If you targeted yourself with this ability, only you become Supercharged.

Cooldown: Moderate (Roughly 16-12s)
Mana Cost: Moderate

E - Rush

Select a location and charge at a moderate pace towards it, destroying projectiles which hit you from the front during the charge. Enemy champions hit during the charge take damage and are slowed significantly. The dash ends upon hitting a wall. If you are Supercharged at the time of casting, instead cast Empowered Rush.

Empowered Rush: Select a location and charge at a moderate pace towards it, destroying projectiles which hit you from the front during the charge. Enemy champions hit during the charge are damaged and carried with you the rest of the distance. The dash ends upon hitting a wall, throwing carried champions over it.

Cooldown: High (Roughly 24-16s)
Mana Cost: High

R - United Charge

Hold down the cast button to charge for up to 3s, then release to perform a dash towards the cursor, carrying the closest ally within a short range with you. The range of the dash increases over the first 1.5s of charging. Both you and the carried ally are untargetable during the dash.

If the dash hits an enemy champion, it stops immediately and creates an explosion which damages nearby enemies. After the dash, both you and the carried ally are Supercharged if you end the dash near at least one enemy champion. If you were already Supercharged, the duration is instead refreshed.

Cooldown: Moderate (Roughly 90-60s)
Mana Cost: Moderate
